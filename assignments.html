Conflict & Peace

Peace 101

This class introduces core theories and practices related to conflict and peace. It explores key principles, tensions, and dynamics that are fundamental to conflict and teaches students how to engage conflict in collaborative problem-solving ways. This class is designed to enhance capacity for creative conflict transformation in multiple contexts, including families and workplaces as well as local, national, and global communities.

  • What Conflict & Peace Mean to Me
  • Overcoming Our Fear of Conflict
  • Conflict Views & Styles
  • Types of Violence
  • Transforming Conflict
  • Intervening in Conflicts
  • Community Conflict Analysis
  • What Conflict & Peace Now Mean to Me

Agile Project management

CS 160

Learn to develop software quickly and reliably using Scrum, the popular agile framework used in the software development industry. Students participate in Scrum teams, taking on various roles and implementing software engineering best practices. Students create and use Scrum artifacts such as the Product Backlog and burndown charts.

Intro to Project Management

PM 140

Learn the fundamental principles of project management, including scope, schedule, and budget. The course covers additional concepts such as stakeholder management, risk assessment, managing expectations, change control, and project closure. Through the application of a real-world scenario, you will gain practical experience and become familiar with project management practices.

Introduction to Supply Chain Management and Operations

SCM 125

Theories and practices of supply chain management and operations. Topics explored and practiced in this course include sales and operations planning (S&OP), negotiation, quality, distribution & logistics, Theory of Constraints (TOC), and lean manufacturing. Students will gain an understanding of the goals and impacts of supply chain management and develop skills to apply in an organization.
